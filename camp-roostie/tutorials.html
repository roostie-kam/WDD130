Education

The most effective way to plan for temperature and weather changes in the High Uintas is to be prepared for both hot & dry and cold & wet weather. A dry shirt and shorts, long sleeve cotton shirt and pants will keep you cooler on hot days. Button down shirts provide more sun protection on the neck, and a light weight pair of pants. Proper eyewear used for hiking and good shoes to protect your feet. Bring something comfortable to wear around camp and to sleep in. A water filter, frame pack for hiking, lotion, headlamp or flashlight, moleskin, and sunscreen.
