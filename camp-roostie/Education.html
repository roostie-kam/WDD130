Utah Camping

Kings Peak, Utah

Winding through the majestic Henry's Fork trail in eastern Utah, in some of the most desolate land anywhere in the state, the High Uintas offers beautiful scenery, and great rapids have made this river run well-known. This 26-mile trip runs from the Henry's Fork trailhead to the summit and it boasts some outstanding scenery, fascinating scree formations, and provides great camping and hiking opportunities. Kings Peak is rated as a the most challenging of the lower 48 high points and can be typically done in 2 days Kings Peak is recognized as a National State High Point. This designation is based on its difficulty and rich history that the Uintas tells.

Payson Canyon, Utah

Camping in Payson Canyon combines world-class scenery to make one truly unforgettable experience. The canyon is filled not only with an abundance of areas to camp. The Canyon can accommodate such a wide variety of skill.
