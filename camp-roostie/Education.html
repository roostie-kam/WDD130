Utah County

Kings Peak, Utah

Winding through the majestic Henry's Fork trail in eastern Utah, in some of the most desolate land anywhere in the state, the High Uintas offer beautiful scenery, and great lakes and rivers that have made the area well-known. This 26-mile trip runs from the Henry's Fork trailhead to the summit and it boasts some outstanding scenery, fascinating scree formations, and provides great camping and hiking opportunities. Kings Peak is rated as a the most challenging of the lower 48 high points and can be typically done in 2 days and Kings Peak is recognized as a National State High Point. This designation is based on its difficulty and rich history that the Uintas tells. This is a summit I'm doing in summer 2023.

Payson Canyon, Utah

This is the grand daddy of all canyons and the best of all areas to camp in Utah. You will never forget your visit to Payson Canyon. Camping in Payson Canyon combines world-class scenery to make one truly unforgettable experience. The canyon is filled not only with an abundance of areas to camp, and can accommodate a wide variety of skill.
